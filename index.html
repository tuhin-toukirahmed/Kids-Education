Standard Curriculum

Kinder (3-6) Years

In kindergarten, children learn basics like letters, sounds, and numbers. They start writing simple words and sentences. They explore the world through science, observing plants and animals. Social skills are emphasized. Motor skills, both fine and gross, are developed through various activities and play.

Elementary School

Elementary school students hone foundational skills, such as reading fluently and comprehending texts. They write structured sentences and paragraphs. Math lessons cover addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Science experiments introduce earth science and biology.

Middle (10-16) Years

In middle school, students deepen their understanding of core subjects. They engage in more complex reading and writing, crafting essays and analyzing texts. Math includes algebra and geometry. Science expands to chemistry, physics, and biology. Social studies cover history, government, and cultures.
